Bugando Medical Centre envisions a healthy society served by skilled and competent health care providers.

Bugando Medical Centre as a tertiary referral hospital exists to improve the welfare of the community in the Lake Zone through provision of state of the art, diversified, specialised and super specialised health care, research, training and consultancy services guided by Catholic Church ethics and morals.

  1. We are committed and entrusted to serve all people with integrity, compassion, and love.
  2. We are non-profit making health institution committed to providing health services that are affordable and accessible to all.
  3. We protect and provide the dignity of human life. We serve all people irrespective of religious, tribal, social standing or political background.
  4. we share and work in  partnership with others  who share our vision and mission.
  5. we abide with the word of God  as an inspiration of all our activities and remain committed  to medical and Roman catholic ethics and regulation.
  6. The Hospital is owned by Tanzania Episcopal Conference and works in collaboration with the Government of United Republic of Tanzania. 
  7. We are committed to team work, transparency and zero tolerance to theft, corruption and fraud.

Wound Management Short-course Form

Course Title: Wound Management
Course Objectives

To equip Nurses with the knowledge and attributes essential for the provision of safe and effective wound management to clients.

Topics to be Covered
  • Wound assessment and classification
  • Wound assessment principles, the standard wound dressing procedures, and techniques used in wound management.
  • Alternative wound dressing and materials.
Learning Outcomes
  • Apply wound dressing principles, standards operating procedures, and techniques in wound dressing procedures.
  • Utilize alternative wound dressing materials in managing wounds.
Eligible Candidates
  • Nurses or Clinicians
  • Must have an active license from respective registering councils, such as TNMC or MCT.
  • Nurses working or expecting to work in fields related to wound management.
Teaching Modality
  • Face-to-face facilitation
  • Procedure Observation
  • Direct practice with patients
  • Course assessment

The course is planned for 5 days from 9th to 13th September, 2024, and will be held within the Bugando Premises.

Internal Medicine

Bugando Medical Centre has new and renovated Private and VIP wings which caters for patients who would prefer to have such services. The Private Wing has 38 beds including a 5 beds High Dependence Unit (HDU) with 24 hours critical nursing services. There are 5 rooms, each room containing 5 beds that are well partitioned for privacy and share 2 bathrooms and toilets. The rooms are all equipped with an alarm system to call for any help or assistance from a nursing station. The Private Wing has also Mini-VIP rooms that contain 2 beds with 1 bathroom and a toilet, Television, fridge and a water heater. Medical services include daily specialist rounds and nursing services. The VIP wing has 18 rooms and each room has 2 beds for the patient and his or her relative. The rooms are furnished with a TV, air condition and a free WiFi. Medical services include daily medical specialist round, around the clock nursing services as well as fast tracked laboratory and imaging services. NEPHROLOGY UNIT The burden of renal diseases in Tanzania is growing, as in sub-Saharan Africa. Non communicable diseases are a driving force of chronic kidney disease. Acute kidney injury is also common with increased morbidity and mortality. Hence a significant need exists for disease prevention with pro-active screening and diagnosis, to provide early treatment to high risk populations across the country.Additionally, existing advanced renal failure needs treatment with dialysis and or transplantation. Implementation of the current knowledge can substantially reduce kidney disease burden and improve patient SUCCESS Nephrology unit is the fastest growing unit in terms of having exponential raise in number of patients as well as hemodialysis patients who are cumulatively increasing with minimum number of discharge. We started hemodialysis with 3 three patients in July 2016 and we have cared since then more than 456 patients and currently we have 95 patients doing maintenance/chronic hemodialysis at our centre. And we started with 10 machines we have expanded the unit and currently we have 23 machines including infectious unit, and we are using new modern machines. Our nephrology clinic is taking care of approximately 600 patients with different renal diseases per month who are on different stages of renal failure, expecting that at one time they will need hemodialysis as a bridge to the renal transplant as a definite treatment. VISION To provide personalize sustainable patient care with compassion and dignity that is supported by dedicated health care professionals, medical innovation and collaboration. We are growing to be independent department and eventually Bugando Institute of Nephrology MISSION To be the choice provider of specialized kidney diseases and dialysis services. Our motto MAKE IT HAPPEN. Permanent access Currently we are doing Permanent central line catheterization, soon we are going to start Renal transplant.

General Surgery

Introduction The Department of General Surgery is the mother Department for surgical services, as since the beginning of the Bugando regional hospital services, it is one of the four branches of medical services, including internal medicine, children's services and maternal and reproductive health services (Obstetric and Gynecology). At present, the Surgical services are divided into several Departments and one of them is General Surgery after the creation of other surgical services departments including bones, air system and ears, nervous system and brain, urinary system, lungs and heart and eyes. All these departments are under the directorate of surgery. Service status in the Department of General Surgery At the moment, the General Surgery Service has a total of 12 specialists and two others are in general surgery training. The Department of General Surgery has a Pediatric surgery unit with two general surgeons and one in training. The Department of General Surgery provides clinical services to more than 1,200 patients and performs more than 220 surgeries per month. These patients are mostly those who were referred from hospitals in the lake region and the western region with a total of seven regions. These patients are in different conditions, some with diseases that have failed in the regional care centers, although others are in normal condition for routine surgery services. The Department of General Surgery provides services in collaboration with other departments to complete diagnosis and treatment. Some of those important units are; Laboratory for tests of blood, urine, toilet and other body fluids samples, including the study of the type of parasites in wounds. Radiology for the diagnosis of diseases including tumors in various parts. Oncology department for cancer treatment before and after surgery for cancerous tumors Physiotherapy department for post-operative exercise care The nutrition unit in planning the nutrition of our patients before and after surgery Services provided by the Department of General Surgery The Department of General Surgery serves its patients in 4 main areas Surgical clinic; These clinics are run by expert doctors and experts in General Surgery to see all patients who have been referred, those who came without a referral and patients who arrive to continue health monitoring after surgery. General surgery wards; patients who need close attention or surgery are admitted and seen by teams of doctors led by specialist doctors and specialists Operating rooms; patients who need major and minor surgery are provided with these services in three rooms designated for non-emergency surgery and also in emergency rooms for emergency patients. Wallet Service; a team of specialist doctors and specialists provide surgical services in hospitals in the lake region and in the west according to schedules arranged at the beginning of the year according to the needs of the respective areas. Currently, the surgery department performs surgery for the following diseases Intestinal obstruction (Intestinal obstruction) Intestinal and gastric perforation Intestinal fistula (intestinal fistula) Intra-abdominal tumors Tumors outside the body (sarcomas, lipoma, melanoma, ganglions ect) Breast diseases (breast diseases and cancers) Diseases of the neck gland (Goitre) Tumors of the food system (gastric tumors, colonic tumors, rectal tumors) Pancreatic tumors Splenic masses and injuries Liver masses and laceration Stones and tumors of the gall system (cholelithiasis and biliary tumors) Various wounds (wounds and ulcers) Hernias Haemorrhoids Anal fissure and fistula Prepared by the Head of the Department of General Surgery

Gastroenterology

ENDOSCOPY UNIT Introduction Endoscopy unit is dedicated for upper gastrointestinal and lower intestinal endoscopy that is, oesophago-gastro-duodenoscopy and colonoscopy procedures; which is minimally invasive for both medical and surgical procedures utilizing instrument so called endoscope which has a camera at the tip end for magnifying of an image to be projected onto a video screen for viewing and recording. Endoscopic procedures they include diagnostic and intervention. The diagnostic procedures are oesophago-gastro-duodenoscopy (OGD) and colonoscopy. Intervention procedures involve oesophageal variceal ligation, sclerotherapy, foreign body removal, biopsy taking, dilation etc. Strengthening the endoscopy unit The unit is now well equipped with gastroscopes, colonoscopies, forceps biopsies, band ligators, processors and other endoscopic accessories which allowing a numbered of procedures to be performed. The number of procedures has increased significantly from 847 last quarter that is from October to December, 2022 to 1,069 in January to March, 2023 Moreover, the unit has introduced newly procedures; fibroscan and elastrography for assessing variety of liver diseases.

Neurosurgery

The Department of Neurosurgery at Bugando Medical Center currently consist of 4 Neurosurgeons and 3 registrars. Our team is committed to excellence in teaching and delivery of quality clinical neurosurgical care for patients with disorders of brain, spine and for patients with traumatic brain and spinal cord injuries. The clinical service serves a population in the lake zone region but also stretches out to the borders of Uganda, Congo, Burundi and Kenya as the main referral center for neurosurgical conditions and management. The department does over 600 surgical cases annually and offers the full breadth of neurosurgery lest of stereotactic and neuro-interventional procedures an area yet to be developed. Our experienced team of surgeons from Tanzania, Uganda and Cuba work collaboratively with neurologists, oncologists, pediatricians and a team of trained nurses to manage the complex delicate care of neurosurgery patients. RESEARCH The department of neurosurgery at BMC has a long tradition of integrating academic based principles into clinical evidence-based practice, and in the past years, several clinical publications especially in areas of head trauma and neuro-tube defects have contributed to the neurosurgical literature. Today the department is actively focused on research in hydrocephalus treatment outcomes, traumatic brain injury and degenerative spine conditions. International Collaborations The department boasts of several international collaborations with some of the best neurosurgical institutions worldwide, including world-known neurosurgeons who visit annually at different times of the year. These have contributed enormously to the advancement of neurosurgical services through exchange of knowledge, expertise, performing complex surgeries though surgical camps and donating surgical equipment such as implants. RESEARCH UNIT

A Unit was established in 2021 in accordance with Bugando Medical Centre (BMC) strategical plan to Promote Research activities, consultancy services and data management and utilization for informed decision making. The key responsibilities of the Unit include planning, resource organization, monitoring, implementation, and coordination of research activities and consultancy services as per international standards guideline. Role of the R&C Unit 1. Develop and sustain the research capacity of the hospital. 2. Co-ordinate and harmonize research activities going on in the various directorates of the hospital 3. Ensure that the Hospital engages in research activities shall be of benefit to patients, researchers, and the scientific community. 4. Ensure provision of evidence-based knowledge that will contribute to the improvement of clinical and non-clinical services in BMC. 5. Ensure that research that is carried out in the hospital is under this governance document, relevant Ministry of Health regulations, and has received ethical clearance. 6. Facilitate the identification and conduct of relevant clinical and non-clinical research and promote the development and testing of new products/tools for use in the hospital. 7. Explore alternatives to current management practices in the hospital by drawing attention to the knowledge that will generalize to the Health Delivery Service in BMC as a whole. 8. Conduct and promote critical review of publications and other information from reliable sources for use by BMC to improve management practice and /or service delivery. 9. Promote and enhance the use of health research results for planning, policy, and decision-making. 10. Promote the dissemination of research findings, including publication of scientific literature by BMC staff. 11. Attract research partnership to BMC, collaborate with external researchers, and improve BMC'S R&C national/international linkages including Publications of scientific literature by BMC staff. Partners and Funding Collaborations with development partners are essential in enabling research and researchers to achieve their goals and objectives. BMC has formed strong collaborations and partnerships with local and international agencies such as Tanzania Ministry of Health, Catholic University of Health and Allied Science, Tanzania National Institute for Medical Research, Duke University, Weill Cornell Medical College, and Wurzburg University. With these partnerships, BMC has mobilized funds and technical assistance to carry out research, train researchers and PhDs, and network within the research world.
